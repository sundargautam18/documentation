Every piece of artwork on Annapurna Art has its own detail page. This is where you'll find the information you need to decide whether to purchase a piece, save it to your wishlist, or learn more about the artist who created it.

## What an artwork page typically includes

<Info>
  Individual artwork pages exist on Annapurna Art and are reachable from category listings, search results, and artist profiles. The exact layout and fields shown below are typical for marketplaces like this one, but haven't been independently verified field-by-field.
</Info>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/buyer-guide/viewing-artwork-details.png" alt="Individual artwork detail page showing an image, title, price, and description" />
</Frame>

An artwork detail page generally includes:

- **Images** of the piece, often with the ability to zoom in or view multiple angles
- **Title** of the artwork
- **Artist name**, usually linking to their full profile
- **Price**
- **Description**, which may cover the medium, inspiration, or story behind the piece
- **Dimensions and medium**, such as size, materials, or technique used
- **Availability**, indicating whether the piece is still for sale
- Actions such as adding the piece to your [wishlist](/buyer-guide/adding-to-wishlist) or starting a purchase

<Note>
  Which of these fields actually appear, and in what order, hasn't been confirmed against the live site. Treat this as a general guide to what to look for rather than an exact page layout.
</Note>

## Viewing the artist's profile

Most artwork pages link directly to the artist's profile, where you can see their other available work, and potentially a short bio or artist statement.

<Tip>
  If you like a particular piece, checking the artist's full profile is a good way to find similar work or discover their broader portfolio.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I see more images of a piece before buying?">

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I know if a piece is still available?">
    Availability status is typically shown directly on the artwork page. If a piece has sold, it's usually marked as unavailable or removed from active listings.
