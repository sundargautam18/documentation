Once you've found a piece you want to own, Annapurna Art lets you purchase it directly from the artist through the platform. This page gives you a high-level overview of the buying process; for the detailed step-by-step checkout flow, see [Checkout Process](/buyer-guide/checkout-process).

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact steps, screens, payment options, and timeframes have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

## From artwork page to purchase

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find and review the artwork">
    Browse or search for a piece, then open its detail page to review the images, description, price, and artist information. See [Viewing Artwork Details](/buyer-guide/viewing-artwork-details).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm availability">
    Check that the piece is still marked as available for sale. Original artwork is one-of-a-kind, so a piece can typically only be purchased by one collector.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the purchase">
    Look for a purchase action on the artwork page, such as "Buy Now" or "Add to Cart," and select it to begin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete checkout">
    Follow the checkout flow to provide shipping and payment details and confirm your order. See [Checkout Process](/buyer-guide/checkout-process) for a detailed walkthrough.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive confirmation">
    After completing your purchase, you should receive an order confirmation, typically by email or on-screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Because artwork on Annapurna Art is original and one-of-a-kind, a piece typically becomes unavailable to other collectors as soon as it's purchased (or, on some marketplaces, as soon as it's added to another buyer's cart). This behavior hasn't been independently confirmed for Annapurna Art specifically.
</Info>

## What happens after you buy

After your purchase, the artist is typically responsible for preparing and shipping the piece. Shipping costs on Annapurna Art are paid by the collector, not the artist. See [Shipping Information](/buyer-guide/shipping-information) for what's confirmed about this.

<Tip>
  Keep an eye on your email and account for updates about your order's status. See [Order Tracking](/buyer-guide/order-tracking) for more on this.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can two collectors buy the same piece of artwork?">
    Original artwork is one-of-a-kind, so this is unlikely by design. The exact mechanism Annapurna Art uses to prevent this (such as reserving a piece once checkout begins) hasn't been independently verified.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I cancel an order after purchasing?">
    Not confirmed. If you need to cancel or change an order, see [Contact Support](/support/contact-support) as soon as possible after purchasing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need an account to purchase artwork?">
    Most marketplaces require an account to complete a purchase, even if browsing is available without one. See [Creating an Account](/getting-started/creating-an-account) if you haven't signed up yet.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
