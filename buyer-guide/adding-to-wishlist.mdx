title: "Adding to Wishlist"
description: "Learn how to save artwork to your wishlist on Annapurna Art so you can revisit it later."
---

Annapurna Art includes a Wishlist feature that lets you save artwork you're interested in, so you can find it again without having to search or browse for it a second time.

<Info>
  The Wishlist feature is confirmed to exist on Annapurna Art and is referenced across product pages. The exact button label, icon, and workflow shown below haven't been independently verified — check the live artwork page for the current design.
</Info>

## Adding a piece to your wishlist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a piece you're interested in">
    Browse or search for artwork, then open the piece's detail page. See [Browsing Artwork](/buyer-guide/browsing-artwork) or [Searching & Filtering](/buyer-guide/searching-filtering) if you haven't found one yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the wishlist action">
    On the artwork's detail page, look for a wishlist action, typically represented by a heart icon or a labeled button such as "Add to Wishlist."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the wishlist action">
    Selecting it should save the piece to your wishlist. You may need to be signed in for this action to work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm it was saved">
    Some marketplaces show a confirmation message or change the icon's appearance (for example, filling in a heart icon) to indicate the piece was saved successfully.
  </Step>
</Steps>

  <img src="/images/buyer-guide/adding-to-wishlist.png" alt="Artwork detail page with a wishlist icon highlighted" />

<Note>
  If you're not signed in, you may be prompted to log in or create an account before you can add a piece to your wishlist. See [Creating an Account](/getting-started/creating-an-account) if you don't have one yet.
</Note>

## Viewing your wishlist

Once you've saved pieces, you can typically find them together in one place, often under your account menu.

<Tip>
  For a closer look at managing everything you've saved, see [Wishlist](/account-management/wishlist) in the account management section.
</Tip>

## Removing a piece from your wishlist

If you no longer want to track a piece, you can usually remove it the same way you added it: by selecting the wishlist action again on the artwork page or from within your wishlist view.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need an account to use the wishlist?">
    This is likely, since a wishlist typically needs to be tied to your account to persist across visits. This hasn't been independently confirmed, but it's the typical pattern for marketplaces like this one.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit to how many pieces I can save?">
    Not confirmed. Most marketplaces don't impose a strict limit, but check the live site if you run into any restrictions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does adding a piece to my wishlist reserve it for purchase?">
    No. A wishlist is generally just a way to track pieces you're interested in — it doesn't reserve the item or guarantee availability when you're ready to buy.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
