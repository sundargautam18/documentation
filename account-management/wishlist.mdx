Annapurna Art includes a Wishlist feature that lets you save artwork you're interested in and revisit it later. This page covers managing the items already in your wishlist. If you haven't added anything yet, see [Adding to Wishlist](/buyer-guide/adding-to-wishlist).

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact steps, screens, field names, and options have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

<Info>
The Wishlist feature itself is confirmed to exist on Annapurna Art and is referenced across product pages. The exact icons, buttons, and management options described below haven't been independently verified — check the live site for the current design.
</Info>

## Viewing your wishlist

Your saved items are typically collected in one place, usually accessible from your account menu after you log in.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/account-management/wishlist.png" alt="Wishlist page showing saved artwork with options to remove items or move them to cart" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your account">
    You'll generally need to be signed in to view your saved items. See [Logging In](/getting-started/logging-in) if you need to sign in first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open your wishlist">
    From your account menu, select the option for your wishlist. It's often represented by a heart icon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your saved items">
    Your wishlist should display the artwork you've saved, typically with the title, artist, price, and availability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing saved items

Once you're viewing your wishlist, you can typically manage each item in a few ways.

<Note>
The exact options available for each saved item haven't been independently verified. Based on common patterns for marketplaces like this one, you can typically expect to:
</Note>

- **Remove an item** — take a piece off your wishlist if you're no longer interested, usually by selecting the same heart or wishlist icon again, or a dedicated remove button.
- **Move an item toward purchase** — some marketplaces let you go directly from a wishlist item to checkout or add it to your cart. See [Purchasing Artwork](/buyer-guide/purchasing-artwork) if you're ready to buy a saved piece.
- **Check availability** — since original artwork is often a single, unique piece, an item you saved may sell before you decide to buy it. Your wishlist may indicate if a piece is no longer available.

<Tip>
Because most listings on Annapurna Art are one-of-a-kind originals, don't wait too long to act on a wishlist item you're serious about — it could be purchased by another collector.
</Tip>
