Your profile settings control the personal information Annapurna Art uses to identify you, whether you're a collector or an artist. This page covers the fields you'll typically find and how to update them.

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact steps, screens, field names, and options have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

## Finding your profile settings

Profile settings are usually accessible from your account menu after you log in. See [User Dashboard](/getting-started/user-dashboard) for a general overview of where account-related pages tend to live.

## Typical profile fields

<Info>
The exact fields, labels, and layout on Annapurna Art haven't been independently verified. Based on common patterns for marketplaces like this one, your profile typically includes options such as:
</Info>

- **Name** — the name displayed on your account and, for artists, on your public artist profile.
- **Email address** — used for login and account notifications.
- **Avatar or profile photo** — an image shown alongside your name in reviews, comments, or your artist profile.
- **Bio** — for artists, a short description of yourself and your work, usually shown on your public artist page.

<Note>
Collectors and artists may see different fields. Artists typically have additional profile options related to their public-facing artist page, such as a bio, location, or links to external portfolios. This hasn't been confirmed for Annapurna Art specifically.
</Note>

## Updating your profile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile settings">
    From your account menu, select the option for profile or account settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit the fields you want to change">
    Update your name, email, avatar, bio, or other available fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Look for a save or update button to confirm your changes. Some fields, like email, may require additional verification before the change takes effect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If you change your email address, you may need to verify the new address before it becomes active. Check for a confirmation email after saving.
</Tip>
