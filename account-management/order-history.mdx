Your order history is generally where you can look back at artwork you've purchased on Annapurna Art. This page covers what viewing your order history typically looks like.

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact steps, screens, field names, and options have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

<Info>
What's confirmed is that artists update delivery status for an order through the Supplier Dashboard once a piece ships or is delivered. Whether collectors see an equivalent order history screen with the same level of detail has not been directly confirmed, though it's a standard feature for a marketplace like this to offer.
</Info>

## Viewing your order history

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your account">
    See [Logging In](/getting-started/logging-in) if you need to sign in first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open your order history">
    From your account menu, select the option for orders or order history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your past orders">
    You'll typically see a list of orders you've placed, potentially including the artwork purchased, date, price, and status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an order for more detail">
    Some marketplaces let you open an individual order to see more information, such as shipping or delivery status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

  <img src="/images/account-management/order-history.png" alt="Order history page listing past artwork purchases with status and date" />
</Frame>

<Note>
The exact fields shown for each order, and whether live shipping status is included, haven't been independently verified. For what's known about tracking a specific order's shipping status, see [Order Tracking](/buyer-guide/order-tracking).
</Note>

<Tip>
If an order in your history looks incorrect, or you can't find an order you expect to see, don't assume it was lost — check with support before reordering or disputing a charge.
</Tip>
