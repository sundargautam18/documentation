If you're an artist selling on Annapurna Art, you may be wondering how to get your work included in a virtual exhibition or a featured collection. This page gives a generic, heavily hedged overview of how participation often works on art marketplaces — it is not a confirmed description of Annapurna Art's process.

<Warning>
**This section is largely unverified.** Public research turned up only a marketing reference to "virtual exhibitions" and featured/trending artwork on Annapurna Art — the underlying mechanics, curation process, and participation requirements described below are a best-effort, generic description of how exhibition features typically work on art marketplaces. Confirm every detail on this page against the live site (or with the Annapurna Art team) before publishing.
</Warning>

## What we don't know

None of the following has been confirmed for Annapurna Art specifically:

- Whether there's an application or nomination process for exhibitions.
- Whether inclusion is manual (curated by staff), automatic (based on engagement, sales, or recency), or some combination.
- Whether there are eligibility requirements, such as account standing, number of listings, or artwork quality guidelines.
- Whether there's any cost or fee associated with being featured.
- Whether exhibitions run on a schedule you could plan around.

<Note>
Because none of this is confirmed, the sections below describe common patterns from art marketplaces in general. Verify each point against the live Annapurna Art site, or ask the Annapurna Art team directly, before relying on it.
</Note>

## How this typically works on art marketplaces

In general, artist inclusion in a curated exhibition or featured collection tends to follow one or more of these patterns:

- **Staff curation** — a team member selects artwork that fits an exhibition's theme, often reaching out to the artist directly or pulling from active listings.
- **Engagement-based inclusion** — artwork that receives strong views, saves, or sales may be surfaced automatically in a trending or featured section.
- **Artist-initiated requests** — some marketplaces let artists apply, nominate their own work, or ask to be considered for an upcoming exhibition.

## What you can do in the meantime

<Tip>
The most reliable path is to ask directly. [Contact Support](/support/contact-support) to ask whether Annapurna Art currently runs an application process, nomination process, or other opportunity for artists to be considered for a virtual exhibition or featured collection.
</Tip>

While you wait to hear back, a few things are generally good practice for any artist hoping to gain visibility on a marketplace, regardless of whether they map to a specific exhibition program:

- Keep your portfolio complete and up to date. See [Portfolio Management](/artist-guide/portfolio-management).
- Make sure each listing has clear photos, an accurate description, and thoughtful pricing. See [Uploading Artwork](/artist-guide/uploading-artwork) and [Artwork Pricing](/artist-guide/artwork-pricing).
- Check your [Artist Dashboard](/artist-guide/artist-dashboard) periodically for any announcements or opportunities related to exhibitions or featured artwork.
