<Warning>
**This section is largely unverified.** Public research turned up only a marketing reference to "virtual exhibitions" and featured/trending artwork on Annapurna Art — the underlying mechanics, curation process, and participation requirements described below are a best-effort, generic description of how exhibition features typically work on art marketplaces. Confirm every detail on this page against the live site (or with the Annapurna Art team) before publishing.

## What a virtual exhibition typically is

On many online art marketplaces, a virtual exhibition is a curated, online-only grouping of artwork presented together around a shared idea, such as a theme, medium, region, or a spotlight on one or more artists. Instead of visiting a physical gallery, you browse the collection through the website, often with additional context like an introduction, artist notes, or curator commentary.

<Note>
Whether Annapurna Art's virtual exhibitions include curator commentary, run on a fixed schedule, or rotate periodically has not been confirmed. Treat the description on this page as a general model of how the feature likely works, not a confirmed specification.
</Note>

## How you might browse a virtual exhibition

If Annapurna Art's virtual exhibitions follow common marketplace patterns, you would typically:

- Find a link or banner to current exhibitions from the homepage or a dedicated navigation entry.
- Open the exhibition to see a themed grouping of artwork, possibly with an introductory description.
- Click through to individual pieces to view full details, pricing, and the artist's profile.
- Save pieces you like to your wishlist for later, the same way you would with any other artwork on the site.

<Tip>
For details on viewing an individual piece once you find it inside an exhibition, see [Viewing Artwork Details](/buyer-guide/viewing-artwork-details). To keep track of pieces you like, see [Adding to Wishlist](/buyer-guide/adding-to-wishlist).
</Tip>

## How this differs from regular browsing

Regular category and theme browsing on Annapurna Art shows you the full breadth of what's available in a given area. A virtual exhibition, by contrast, is typically a smaller, hand-picked selection intended to tell a more focused story, such as a group of artists working in a similar style or a set of pieces connected to a particular idea.

<Info>
If you'd rather explore the full catalog instead of a curated selection, see [Browsing Artwork](/buyer-guide/browsing-artwork) and [Searching & Filtering](/buyer-guide/searching-filtering).
</Info>
