---

Setting the right price for your artwork is one of the most important parts of listing a piece on Annapurna Art. This page covers how pricing generally works and, importantly, what has not yet been confirmed.

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact pricing rules, currency handling, and interface for setting a price have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Commission status: unconfirmed.** Whether Annapurna Art takes a commission or fee on artist sales — or whether artists keep 100% of the sale price — has not been confirmed either way. Do not assume a specific percentage or assume there is no fee. Verify this directly with Annapurna Art (for example, via [Contact Support](/support/contact-support)) before relying on it for financial planning.
</Warning>

## Setting a price

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the artwork listing">
    While [uploading](/artist-guide/uploading-artwork) or [updating](/artist-guide/updating-artwork) a piece, go to the pricing field in the listing form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your price">
    Enter the price you want to charge for the piece. The exact currency options and any minimum price rules haven't been confirmed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your listing">
    Save or publish the listing with your chosen price.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/artist-guide/artwork-pricing.png" alt="Pricing field within an artwork listing form" />
</Frame>

<Note>
Collectors pay for shipping separately from the artwork price on many marketplaces. Whether this applies on Annapurna Art specifically hasn't been confirmed — see [Shipping Information](/buyer-guide/shipping-information) for the buyer-side view.
</Note>

<Tip>
When pricing your work, consider factors like materials, size, time invested, and prices for comparable pieces by other artists on the platform. Browsing the [artist directory](https://annapurnaart.com/artist) can help you get a sense of typical pricing.
</Tip>

## What's confirmed vs. unconfirmed

| Topic | Status |
| --- | --- |
| Artists can set a price when listing artwork | Assumed based on standard marketplace behavior; exact interface not confirmed |
| Annapurna Art charges artists to join | Confirmed — joining is free |
| Annapurna Art takes a commission on sales | **Unconfirmed** — do not assume a percentage either way |
| Currency used for pricing | Not confirmed |
| Minimum or maximum price rules | Not confirmed |
| Payout methods (once a sale completes) | Confirmed — Khalti or credit card |

<Warning>
Because commission status is unconfirmed, avoid telling artists what they will "net" from a sale. Direct pricing and payout questions to [Contact Support](/support/contact-support) until this is verified.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does Annapurna Art take a cut of my sales?">
    This is not confirmed either way. Don't assume artists keep 100% or that a specific commission applies — check directly with Annapurna Art before making financial decisions based on pricing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I change my price after listing an artwork?">
    Yes, artists can edit and update listings, which should include price. See [Updating Artwork](/artist-guide/updating-artwork).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What currency should I price in?">
    This hasn't been confirmed. Check the pricing field in your listing form for available currency options.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I get paid once my artwork sells?">
    Payment is released once Annapurna Art updates the delivery status on your dashboard to show the order has been delivered. See [Orders & Sales](/artist-guide/orders-and-sales) for the full flow, and payout methods include Khalti and credit card.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
