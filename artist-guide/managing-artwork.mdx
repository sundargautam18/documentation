As you add more pieces to your portfolio, your Supplier Dashboard becomes the place to view and organize everything you have listed for sale on Annapurna Art.

<Warning>
This page describes the typical flow for a marketplace like Annapurna Art. The exact screens, filters, and organizational tools available in the dashboard have not been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review) — confirm against the live site and update this page before publishing.
</Warning>

## Viewing your listings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your dashboard">
    Sign in and go to your [Artist Dashboard](/artist-guide/artist-dashboard).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your artwork section">
    Look for a section listing all the artwork you currently have published, such as "My Artwork" or "My Listings."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review each listing">
    Check each piece's images, details, price, and status (for example, whether it's available, sold, or pending an order).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/artist-guide/managing-artwork.png" alt="Dashboard view of an artist's current artwork listings" />
</Frame>

<Note>
Keeping an eye on which pieces are sold versus still available helps you avoid confusion when collectors reach out or place orders.
</Note>

<Tip>
Periodically review older listings to make sure prices, descriptions, and images are still accurate — especially for pieces that have been up for a while.
</Tip>

## What you can typically do from here

- Review the status of each artwork (available, sold, or pending)
- Open a listing to [update it](/artist-guide/updating-artwork)
- Remove or unpublish a listing that's no longer for sale
- Add a new piece by [uploading artwork](/artist-guide/uploading-artwork)

<Warning>
The specific actions available (such as removing or unpublishing a listing) have not been confirmed. Check your dashboard directly for available options.
</Warning>
