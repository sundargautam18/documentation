Annapurna Art offers a set of features tailored to two audiences: collectors looking to discover and buy original artwork, and artists looking to sell it. This page summarizes what's currently available on the platform.

## Marketplace features

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Marketplace browsing and search | Browse original artwork by category (such as painting, sculpture, photography, or drawing) and by theme or country. See [Browsing Artwork](/buyer-guide/browsing-artwork) and [Searching & Filtering](/buyer-guide/searching-filtering). |
| Artist storefronts | View individual artist profiles and buy original work directly from the artist. |
| Wishlist | Save artwork you're interested in for later. See [Adding to Wishlist](/buyer-guide/adding-to-wishlist). |
| Buyer-paid shipping | Shipping costs are paid by the collector, not the artist. See [Shipping Information](/buyer-guide/shipping-information). |

## Artist features

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Free artist signup | Artists can join and list work at no cost. See [Becoming an Artist](/artist-guide/becoming-an-artist). |
| Supplier Dashboard | A dedicated dashboard for artists to manage their portfolio, respond to sales, and track orders from anywhere in the world. See [Artist Dashboard](/artist-guide/artist-dashboard). |
| Artwork management | Add, edit, and update artwork listings, including uploading up to 5 images per artwork. See [Uploading Artwork](/artist-guide/uploading-artwork) and [Managing Artwork](/artist-guide/managing-artwork). |
| Multiple payout methods | Artists can receive payouts via Khalti (a Nepali digital wallet) or credit card. |

## Exhibitions and featured collections

Annapurna Art also references "virtual exhibitions" and featured or curated collections of artwork as part of the marketplace experience.

<Warning>
  The exact mechanics of exhibitions and featured collections on Annapurna Art haven't been independently verified against the live product (the site blocks automated review). Confirm how this feature works on the live site before publishing detailed guidance. See [Virtual Exhibitions](/exhibitions/virtual-exhibitions) for what's currently documented.
</Warning>
